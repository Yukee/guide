# PG, VG, and Recrystallization

This entry covers the solubility of DMT freebase in propylene glycol (PG) and vegetable glycerin (VG), and the conditions under which dissolved DMT recrystallizes inside vape cartridges. The evidence base is entirely community-sourced — no published study has measured DMT solubility in PG or VG.

## Overview

- **PG is the solvent.** DMT freebase dissolves well in propylene glycol. It dissolves poorly or not at all in vegetable glycerin.
- **VG is a comfort additive.** VG is included in some blends to produce smoother vapor and larger clouds — properties borrowed from nicotine vaping. It does not help dissolve DMT and reduces the solution's capacity to hold DMT in suspension.
- **Recrystallization risk depends primarily on the DMT-to-PG ratio** (how many mg of DMT per ml of PG), with VG content as a secondary risk factor.

## DMT Solubility in PG vs VG

### Community consensus

Multiple experienced users on DMT-Nexus report that DMT dissolves well in PG but poorly in VG:

- **fractals4life** [1]: "It will dissolve well in PG but not in VG, so go with a 50/50 PG/VG or 70/30 or pure PEG400"
- **"some one"** [2] (citing *The Essential DMT Guide* [10]): "DMT dissolves well in PG, not well in VG. 1ml of PG can dissolve 1g DMT"
- **pantostao** [3]: "DMT is way more soluble in PG than VG"
- **Loveall** [4]: "I use zero VG" — reported that VG "only makes DMT and its salt less soluble" and noticed "no improvement in taste with VG"
- **pantostao** [4]: "Same, no VG at all, it is not needed"
- **Hyper Turtle** [5]: "If the PG is pure, and the spice is freebase, it should dissolve with no issues"

One user offered a contrasting chemical claim:

- **Serotonika** [6]: "DMT freebase is non-polar, while PG is polar. Meaning DMT does not dissolve very well into PG."

This contradicts the practical experience reported by all other users above. The chemistry is also oversimplified — DMT freebase has hydrogen-bond donor groups (the indole NH) and is only moderately lipophilic, making PG a reasonable solvent despite the polarity difference. The overwhelming weight of community experience is that pure freebase DMT dissolves readily in PG.

### Why some guides recommend PG/VG blends

Nicotine e-liquid guides commonly recommend 50/50 or 70/30 PG/VG blends because VG produces larger, smoother vapor clouds and reduces the "throat hit" that PG causes. Many DMT vaping guides inherited this convention.

However, for DMT — especially at the low doses used for aborting cluster headache attacks — several experienced users report that VG adds no benefit and only reduces solubility [1][4]. The throat-hit reduction matters less when taking 1–2 short puffs rather than chain-vaping.

### Summary

| Solvent | Dissolves DMT? | Role |
|---|---|---|
| PG (propylene glycol) | Yes — well | Primary solvent |
| VG (vegetable glycerin) | Poorly or not at all | Optional comfort additive (smoother vapor) |
| PEG400 (polyethylene glycol 400) | Reportedly well — possibly better than PG | Uncommon; limited data [1][7] |

## Recrystallization

When DMT is dissolved in PG at concentrations above its room-temperature solubility limit, it forms a supersaturated solution. Over time — or when cooled — the excess DMT precipitates out as crystals. These crystals clog the small intake holes in the cartridge's coil assembly, blocking airflow and liquid delivery.

### Concentration thresholds

The most specific data comes from user **Serotonika** on DMT-Nexus [6]:

> "Below 200 mg/ml the solution is stable and doesn't recrystallise, whereas pushing towards or above 250 mg/ml increases the likelihood of crystallisation."

At 300 mg/ml, Serotonika reports that dissolution is possible with heating, but "once it cools down it will likely recrystallise and clog up."

Other data points:

- **The Neural** [8]: Dissolved at least 1.5 g DMT in 10 ml PG (≥150 mg/ml) at room temperature with no reported recrystallization — consistent with the <200 mg/ml stability claim.
- **mamont** [5]: Used "1g of spice and 2g of liquid for years" (~500 mg/ml in a 50/50 PG/VG blend) — but this required heat to dissolve and is above the stable range.
- **fastfred** [8]: "You need heat to get a super saturated solution."
- **Vodsel** [8]: Warned that supersaturated solutions will "slowly precipitate clogging the device" once cooled.

| Concentration (in PG) | Stability at room temperature | Source |
|---|---|---|
| <200 mg/ml | Stable — no recrystallization expected | Serotonika [6] |
| ~200–250 mg/ml | Transition zone — may or may not recrystallize | Serotonika [6] |
| >250 mg/ml | Increased recrystallization risk | Serotonika [6] |
| >300 mg/ml | Likely to recrystallize without sustained heat | Serotonika [6] |

### Effect of VG on recrystallization

Because DMT dissolves poorly in VG, adding VG to the carrier liquid effectively reduces the amount of solvent (PG) available. This lowers the concentration at which recrystallization occurs.

- **pantostao** [3]: "If you're using a cart, and thus, a high potency liquid (>1:1 ratio) I'd recommend not using any VG, because DMT will recrystallize."
- A thread on vape cartridge solutions [9] notes: "VG promotes crystal's building up in the cart."

The practical implication: the recrystallization thresholds in the table above assume pure PG. If the carrier liquid contains VG, the effective thresholds are lower — how much lower depends on the VG fraction, but no quantitative data exists on this relationship.

### Effect of temperature

- **Cold promotes recrystallization.** Solubility decreases at lower temperatures. Cartridges stored in cold environments (car glovebox in winter, refrigerator) are more likely to clog. Room temperature storage (18–25°C / 65–77°F) is recommended. See [vaporization-and-preparation.md](vaporization-and-preparation.md) for clearing clogs.
- **Heat enables supersaturation.** Warming a solution allows more DMT to dissolve than would stay in solution at room temperature. This is useful during preparation (hot water bath to dissolve crystals) but means the solution may be supersaturated at room temperature, leading to gradual recrystallization.

### What the thresholds mean in practice

The relevant variable for recrystallization is **mg of DMT per ml of PG** (not per ml of total liquid). In a 50/50 PG/VG blend, only the PG fraction acts as solvent. So a "333 mg/ml" solution made with 50/50 PG/VG effectively loads ~666 mg of DMT per ml of PG — far above the stable threshold. The same 333 mg/ml made with pure PG loads 333 mg/ml of PG — still above the stable threshold, but much less so.

This is why experienced users recommend pure PG or high-PG blends for concentrated DMT solutions.

## When DMT Won't Dissolve at All

Some users report that their DMT will not dissolve in PG even at low concentrations and with heat. Community diagnosis points to the DMT material itself rather than the PG:

- **Impure DMT or polymorph issues**: User mamont [5] repeatedly failed to dissolve DMT ("0.25g spice in 0.5 PG = glue"). Community members (Loveall, fractals4life) suggested the yellowish-orange material was likely DMT polymer — a less soluble polymorph that can form during extraction.
- **Base contamination**: User Sakkadelic [5] proposed that residual base from extraction can interfere with dissolution.
- **Solution**: A "mini A/B" re-extraction or recrystallization from naphtha to obtain pure white freebase DMT typically resolves the issue [5].

Pure, white freebase DMT should dissolve readily in PG at room temperature up to at least 150 mg/ml, and with gentle heating up to much higher concentrations [8].

## PEG400

Polyethylene glycol 400 (PEG400) is occasionally mentioned as an alternative carrier with potentially higher DMT solubility than PG:

- **fractals4life** [1]: Recommended "pure PEG400" as an option and noted it "seems less liable to have DMT crystallise out of it as the tank level drops"
- **Vodsel** [8]: Mentioned PEG400 as a possibly better solvent but with limited verification: "PEG-400? I recall foaf mentioning 'it worked'"

PEG400 is less commonly used and has a thinner evidence base than PG. It is included here for completeness but is not the community standard.

## Evidence Quality Note

All claims in this entry are sourced from harm reduction forums (primarily DMT-Nexus) and attributed to specific users. No published study has measured the solubility of DMT freebase in propylene glycol, vegetable glycerin, or PEG400. The concentration thresholds (<200 mg/ml stable, >250 mg/ml risky) come from a single user's report (Serotonika), though they are broadly consistent with the practical experiences described by other users.

The claim that "1 ml of PG can dissolve 1 g DMT" appears to refer to dissolution with heat, producing a supersaturated solution that may recrystallize at room temperature. It should not be interpreted as the room-temperature solubility limit.
