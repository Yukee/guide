# Aborting with DMT

This entry covers the dosing and delivery specifics relevant to aborting cluster headache attacks with inhaled DMT. It focuses on how much active substance reaches the user per inhalation across different devices and concentrations.

## Aborting Dose Range

Community reports consistently indicate that very small amounts of vaporized DMT — far below recreational thresholds — can abort a cluster headache attack:

- **~3 mg vaporized** is reported as sufficient by one interview subject, who described pain subsiding "almost completely within 3 to 5 seconds" [1]
- **~5 mg** is cited as effective in a separate community summary, with pain stopping "in literally 10–20 seconds" [2]
- The effective aborting range appears to be roughly **3–5 mg**, with most users needing only 1–2 puffs from a vape pen [1][2]
- Bob Wold (president of Clusterbusters) is quoted: "One inhalation will end the attack for most people... It could end that attack in less than a minute" [3]

### Comparison with recreational dose categories

For context, the Erowid dosage chart for smoked/vaporized DMT lists [4]:

| Category | Dose |
|---|---|
| Threshold | 2–5 mg |
| Light | 10–20 mg |
| Common | 20–40 mg |
| Strong | 40–60 mg |

The aborting dose (3–5 mg) falls at or just above Erowid's threshold — the point where effects first become barely noticeable. This is consistent with community descriptions of aborting as a "sub-psychedelic" or "sub-psychonautic" experience with little to no perceptual distortion [2][3].

The DMT community uses the term "breakthrough" (a distinct, immersive psychedelic state) for doses in the 15–30+ mg range depending on method and individual response [5][6]. This is far above the aborting range.

## Vape Cartridge (510-Thread)

This is the recommended method for aborting cluster headache attacks. A 510-thread vape pen with DMT dissolved in PG/VG liquid allows precise, incremental dosing — take one puff, wait, take another if needed. See [vaporization-and-preparation.md](vaporization-and-preparation.md) for equipment and mixing details.

### How much DMT per draw?

The most direct way to estimate per-draw delivery is to determine how much liquid the device consumes per second of inhalation, then multiply by the DMT concentration. No published study has measured this for DMT vape cartridges, but several community members have reported data that allows estimation.

### Community data points

**redbird** (DMT-Nexus [7]) — the only direct weight measurement found:
- Device: Yocan Uni 3.0 battery at 4.0V with a 510 cartridge (~1.5Ω coil, ~10W)
- Cart: 700 mg DMT dissolved in 1 ml PG (40% DMT by weight)
- Method: Weighed cartridge before and after a timed 5-second pull
- Result: **50 mg of liquid consumed in 5 seconds → 10 mg liquid/sec**
- DMT delivered: 50 × 0.40 = **~20 mg DMT in 5 seconds (~4 mg DMT/sec)**

**nan0tEch** (DMT-Nexus [8]) — measured by counting puffs to empty:
- Device: Smok AL85 sub-ohm tank, 0.4Ω coil, 45W, 260°C (note: much higher power than a 510 cart)
- Mix: 370 mg DMT in 2.2 ml 50/50 PG/VG e-liquid (168 mg/ml, 13% DMT by weight)
- Result: **90 puffs consumed entire tank → ~4 mg DMT per puff**
- Implied liquid rate: ~32 mg liquid/puff; at ~3–5 sec/puff → ~6–11 mg liquid/sec

**TeaDaze** (DMT-Nexus [8]) — effect-calibrated:
- Device: Innokin Coolfire IV, 20W, 0.5Ω atomizer (sub-ohm)
- Mix: ~1 g DMT in 1 ml PG (49% DMT by weight)
- Result: Reached their target dose (~25 mg) "in just one hit"
- Implied liquid rate: ~51 mg liquid per hit; at ~5–7 sec → ~7–10 mg liquid/sec

**Qoniaq** (DMT-Nexus [7]) — effect-calibrated experiential scale:
- Device: Yocan Uni 3.0 at 4.0V (same as redbird's device)
- Cart: 700 mg DMT in only 0.3 ml PG (69% DMT by weight — extremely concentrated and viscous)
- Effect scale: 5 sec = threshold, 10 sec = mild, 20 sec = large, 30+ sec = breakthrough
- Implied liquid rate: **only ~1–2 mg/sec** — about 5–10x slower than redbird's cart on the same device
- Explanation: The extreme viscosity of this mixture severely limits wicking speed. This illustrates why very high concentrations are not just unnecessary for aborting but also impractical — the device can't deliver liquid fast enough.

### Deriving a per-second delivery rate

Excluding Qoniaq's outlier (where extreme viscosity throttled wicking), the three remaining data points converge on a liquid consumption rate of roughly **5–10 mg of liquid per second** for standard devices at typical settings. The one direct measurement (redbird, 510 cart at 4.0V) gives 10 mg/sec; the sub-ohm data points are broadly consistent.

Voltage affects consumption rate: higher voltage vaporizes more liquid per second. Most users run 510 carts at 2.5–3.5V. The measured 10 mg/sec was at 4.0V (the high end). A conservative estimate for lower voltages might be 5–7 mg/sec, though no direct measurement exists for these settings.

### Per-draw delivery at 333 mg/ml

At the guide's recommended concentration of 333 mg/ml (1:3 ratio, 1 g DMT per 3 ml vape juice), the DMT fraction by weight is approximately 22.5%. Multiplying by the liquid consumption rate:

**Estimated DMT delivery: ~1–2 mg per second of inhalation**

| Draw duration | Low estimate (~5 mg liq/sec) | High estimate (~10 mg liq/sec) |
|---|---|---|
| 2 seconds | ~2 mg | ~4 mg |
| 3 seconds (short draw) | ~3 mg | ~7 mg |
| 5 seconds (moderate draw) | ~6 mg | ~11 mg |
| 10 seconds (long draw) | ~11 mg | ~22 mg |

The low estimate reflects lower voltage settings (~2.5–3.0V); the high estimate reflects medium-high settings (~3.5–4.0V) based on redbird's measurement.

### Key variables

The table above is derived from a single direct measurement plus supporting data points. Per-draw delivery varies due to:

1. **Draw duration**: The single biggest factor. A 3-second puff vs. a 10-second pull can differ by 3x or more.
2. **Voltage/wattage**: Higher settings vaporize more liquid per second. This is the second biggest factor.
3. **Coil type and condition**: Fresh ceramic coils deliver more consistently than aged or gunked coils.
4. **Liquid viscosity**: Highly concentrated mixtures wick more slowly (see Qoniaq data above). At 333 mg/ml, viscosity is low enough that this should not be a limiting factor.
5. **Inhalation technique**: Deep lung draws absorb more vapor than shallow mouth draws.

### Practical implication for aborting

At 333 mg/ml, **a single short draw (2–3 seconds) delivers roughly 2–7 mg of DMT**, depending on voltage. This covers the reported 3–5 mg aborting range in most scenarios. A second puff a minute later covers remaining cases.

This means concentrations above 500 mg/ml are unnecessary for aborting and introduce both clogging risk and the wicking-speed problem illustrated by Qoniaq's data (see [vaporization-and-preparation.md](vaporization-and-preparation.md) for recrystallization thresholds).

### Vape cartridge for recreational use (context only)

For reference, community reports on breakthrough doses from vape cartridges typically describe needing **2–4+ long hits** from concentrated cartridges (500+ mg/ml), with significant individual variation [6][9]. Some users report breakthroughs with 2 hits from a 1:2 or 1:1 cart; others need 5+ hits [9]. This is included for context — it illustrates how far above the aborting range recreational dosing sits.

## E-Mesh

DMT freebase crystal is weighed on a milligram scale and placed directly on a stainless steel mesh coil in a box mod. The user fires the mod and inhales the full dose in a single 5–7 second pull.

### Dosage chart (from DMT-Nexus e-mesh guide [5])

| Level | Dose |
|---|---|
| Threshold | 5–10 mg |
| Low | 10–15 mg |
| Common (breakthrough range) | 15–30 mg |
| Recommended starting dose | 20 mg |
| Heroic | 50+ mg |

### Efficiency

E-mesh is widely considered the most efficient consumer vaporization method — the mesh heats evenly, and nearly all loaded material is vaporized in a single hit [5]. Community consensus rates it higher than pipe-based methods (Glass Vapor Genie, oil burner, "The Machine"), which lose varying amounts to incomplete vaporization, residue, or combustion [10].

### Why e-mesh is less suitable for aborting

- **Minimum practical loading (~5–10 mg) already meets or exceeds the aborting range.** There is no way to load 3 mg of crystal reliably on a mesh.
- **Single-hit delivery with no titration.** Unlike a vape pen where you can take one puff and wait, the e-mesh delivers the full loaded dose at once.
- **Requires a milligram scale and manual loading** before each use — impractical during a cluster headache attack.

E-mesh is best suited for users seeking full psychedelic experiences with precise dosing. For aborting, the vape cartridge's ability to deliver 1–3 mg increments on demand makes it the better tool.

## Comparing Methods for Aborting

| | Vape cartridge (510-thread) | E-mesh |
|---|---|---|
| **DMT form** | Dissolved in PG/VG liquid | Freebase crystal on mesh |
| **Dose control** | Incremental (~2–7 mg per short draw) | All-at-once (full load in one hit) |
| **Minimum practical dose** | ~2 mg (one brief puff) | ~5 mg (minimum weighable/loadable amount) |
| **Titration** | Easy — puff, wait, repeat | Not possible — entire load vaporized at once |
| **Preparation during attack** | None — pick up and puff | Weigh crystal, load mesh, assemble mod |
| **Portability** | Pocket-sized, inconspicuous | Requires box mod, scale, crystal supply |
| **Efficiency** | Good (some loss to wicking/residue) | Very high (near-complete vaporization) |
| **Best suited for** | Aborting attacks, low-dose use | Full psychedelic experiences, precise dosing |

## Self-Calibration Method

Because per-puff delivery varies by device and technique, one practical approach from the DMT-Nexus community is the **weigh-before-and-after method** [7]:

1. Weigh the cartridge on a milligram scale before use
2. Take a timed puff (e.g., 5 seconds)
3. Weigh the cartridge again
4. Weight difference × concentration = mg of DMT delivered

This lets each user determine their specific device's delivery rate. For cluster headache patients, this calibration step could help establish confidence in dosing before relying on the method during an attack.

## Evidence Quality Note

### Per-draw delivery estimates

The per-draw estimates in this entry are derived from:

- **One direct weight measurement** by user redbird (DMT-Nexus): weighed a 510 cartridge before and after a timed 5-second pull, yielding 10 mg liquid/sec at 4.0V [7]
- **One puff-count measurement** by user nan0tEch (DMT-Nexus): counted 90 puffs to empty a 2.2 ml tank containing 370 mg DMT, yielding ~4 mg DMT/puff [8]
- **Two effect-calibrated reports** (TeaDaze and Qoniaq on DMT-Nexus) where known effects at known concentrations allow reverse-engineering approximate delivery rates [7][8]

The per-second delivery rate is based primarily on redbird's single measurement, with the other data points serving as cross-checks. The consistency across these independent reports (all clustering around 5–10 mg liquid/sec for non-extreme concentrations) provides some confidence, but this remains a small and informal evidence base.

No published pharmacokinetic study has measured per-puff DMT delivery from consumer vape devices. The only clinical trial of inhaled DMT (BMND01, phase I) used pharmaceutical-grade equipment with precise metered dosing, not consumer cartridges [11]. The estimates here should be treated as informed approximations, not precise measurements.

### Aborting dose

The 3 mg figure comes from a single interview subject's self-report [1]. The ~5 mg figure comes from a community summary citing unnamed patient reports [2]. Bob Wold's clinical observations [3] confirm the general pattern (one inhalation sufficient) without specifying milligrams. While consistent with the broader pattern of sub-psychedelic efficacy reported by cluster headache communities, these figures have not been clinically validated. No controlled trial has established an aborting dose for inhaled DMT in cluster headache.
