# Pharmacokinetics of DMT

DMT is characterized by rapid onset, intense but brief effects, and fast clearance — properties that make it unique among psychedelics. Its pharmacokinetic profile varies significantly depending on the route of administration.

## Routes of Administration

### Inhaled (smoked/vaporized)
When inhaled, DMT produces effects "within seconds" with the experience lasting "about five to fifteen minutes" [1]. No controlled clinical pharmacokinetic studies of inhaled DMT have been published as of 2025 — most formal PK data comes from intravenous studies [2]. However, the inhaled route is pharmacokinetically similar to IV in terms of rapid onset and short duration, since both bypass first-pass metabolism.

### Intravenous
The most extensively studied route. Strassman et al.'s landmark studies (1994-1996) used IV bolus doses of 0.05–0.4 mg/kg DMT fumarate [3][4]. A 2025 systematic review of 13 clinical studies found "median time to peak plasma concentration was ~10 min and mean elimination half-life was 9–12 min" for IV administration [2].

### Oral (with MAOIs — ayahuasca)
DMT is orally inactive on its own because it is rapidly degraded by MAO-A in the gut and liver. Ayahuasca combines DMT with MAO inhibitors (harmine, harmaline) that block this degradation, resulting in oral bioavailability with onset at 30-45 minutes and effects lasting 2-4 hours [1][5].

## Onset and Duration Timeline

For inhaled/IV DMT [1][3][4]:
- **0–10 seconds:** First effects noticeable
- **10 sec – 3-5 min:** Peak intensity
- **5–15 min:** Effects fading
- **15–20 min:** Effects essentially resolved
- **20–30 min:** Return to baseline

Strassman (1994) reported that at 0.4 mg/kg IV, "peak effects were noted within 2 minutes" with "near-complete resolution of effects within 20-30 minutes" [3].

## Metabolism and Clearance

DMT is "predominantly metabolized by oxidative deamination via monoamine oxidase type A (MAO-A), followed by formation of indole-3-acetic acid (3-IAA) by non-specific aldehyde dehydrogenases" [2]. MAO-A metabolism is so efficient that the primary metabolite IAA reaches concentrations "59- to 137-fold higher" than parent DMT [2].

Secondary metabolism occurs via CYP2D6 and CYP2C19 in the liver, which "may account for as high as 10–20% of metabolism" for smoked DMT [1][6].

### Clearance values
A systematic review found clearance ranged from 7.5 L/min (intramuscular) to 46.8 L/min (IV infusion) [2]. Terminal elimination half-life ranged from **4.8 to 19.0 minutes** across studies, with "the initial redistribution phase very short, ranging from 10 to 20 min, followed by a longer elimination phase of up to 120 min" [2].

### Effect of MAOIs on clearance
Co-administration with MAOIs dramatically slows clearance. One study found that "premedication of harmine significantly increased the elimination half-life of DMT to 36 minutes" — roughly 3-4x longer than without MAOIs [5][7].

## No Accumulation from Repeated Use

Given the rapid clearance (half-life of minutes), DMT does not accumulate in the body with repeated use. The compound is fully eliminated between doses when used intermittently.

## Tolerance

DMT is unique among classical psychedelics in its resistance to tolerance development with repeated bolus dosing.

### Evidence for lack of tolerance
Strassman et al. (1996) administered IV DMT (0.3 mg/kg) to 13 experienced volunteers **four times at 30-minute intervals**. Result: "tolerance to 'psychedelic' subjective effects did not occur according to either clinical interview or Hallucinogen Rating Scale scores" [4]. This stands in contrast to LSD, which "induces complete tolerance after four consecutive daily doses" [8].

Dos Santos et al. (2012) similarly "observed no tolerance to the subjective effects of two consecutive doses of ayahuasca" [8].

### Differential tolerance
While subjective psychedelic effects showed no tolerance, Strassman et al. found that physiological responses did diminish: "Adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH), prolactin, cortisol, and heart rate responses decreased with repeated DMT administration, although blood pressure did not" [4]. This suggests tolerance develops to some neuroendocrine and cardiovascular effects but not to the psychedelic experience itself.

### Proposed mechanism
One hypothesis involves DMT's unique receptor pharmacology. DMT's "lipophilic nature allows it to cross cellular membranes and bind to intracellular [5-HT₂A] receptors" — a pool unaffected by the surface receptor downregulation that mediates tolerance to other psychedelics [8]. Additionally, unlike LSD and DOI, "DMT does not desensitize serotonin 5-HT₂A receptors in vitro," possibly because it activates the Gq signaling pathway without significantly recruiting β-arrestin2, a protein linked to receptor downregulation [8].

### Caveat: continuous infusion
Recent continuous-infusion studies (Timmermann et al., 2023) have shown "moderate acute tolerance development" during sustained IV infusion, where "subjective effects showed no increase from 30 to 120 minutes despite increasing plasma concentrations" [9]. This suggests that while bolus re-dosing does not produce tolerance, truly continuous exposure may.

## Relevance to Cluster Headache Aborting

For cluster headache patients using inhaled DMT at aborting doses (5-10 mg):
- Effects begin within seconds and resolve within 10-20 minutes
- The compound is fully cleared within minutes
- Repeated use to abort multiple attacks does not produce tolerance to the aborting effect (consistent with Strassman's findings on bolus re-dosing)
- No accumulation occurs between uses
