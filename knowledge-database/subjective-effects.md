# Subjective Effects of DMT

DMT produces a dose-dependent spectrum of effects ranging from subtle body sensations to full immersive psychedelic experiences. This entry covers effects at aborting doses (5-10 mg inhaled), moderate doses, high doses, and the properties of DMT vapor.

## Effects at Aborting Doses (5-10 mg inhaled)

At aborting doses, DMT produces mild effects while maintaining full cognitive function. These doses are well below the hallucinogenic threshold identified in clinical studies (0.2 mg/kg IV, equivalent to ~14 mg for a 70 kg person) [1].

### Physical sensations
- **Body buzz:** A warm, tingling sensation often starting in the chest and spreading outward. Strassman's dose-response study found that low doses (0.05-0.1 mg/kg IV) were "primarily affective and somaesthetic" — meaning body-sensation-based rather than hallucinatory [1].
- **Heaviness:** A feeling of increased gravity or being pressed gently into the seat.
- **Temperature changes:** Some users report feeling cold; others report slight sweating.
- **Other physical effects:** Pupil dilation, slight trembling or numbness, and increased heart rate [1][2].

### Perceptual changes
- Colors may appear more vivid, edges sharper
- Geometric patterns may be visible, especially with eyes closed
- Some users report hearing static or a high-pitched hum or ringing
- These effects are mild — the user remains fully aware of their surroundings, identity, and can talk and think clearly [2][3].

### Emotional effects
- A feeling of calm or mild euphoria is commonly reported
- Anxiety is uncommon at these doses and, when present, brief

### Practical note
Some cluster headache patients report that the body buzz occurs at approximately the dose needed to abort their attacks, making it a useful calibration signal [3].

## Effects at Moderate Doses (10-25 mg inhaled / ~0.1-0.2 mg/kg IV)

At moderate doses, perceptual effects become more pronounced:
- Vivid visual patterns appear with eyes open
- Time distortion becomes noticeable
- Emotional effects intensify
- The transition into the experience may include a "rush" sensation
- Cognitive function remains largely intact but attention shifts inward [1][4]

## Effects at High Doses (>25 mg inhaled / >0.2 mg/kg IV)

### "Breakthrough" experience
At high doses, DMT produces one of the most intense altered states known. Strassman (1994) reported that at 0.2 and 0.4 mg/kg IV, "hallucinogenic effects... included a rapidly moving, brightly colored visual display of images" with "'loss of control' associated with a brief, but overwhelming 'rush,' led to a dissociated state, where euphoria alternated or coexisted with anxiety" [1].

### Visual phenomena
The visual experience progresses through stages: "closed-eye imagery such as colors, infinitely repeated and wavy sheets of patterns, and kaleidoscopic images" to fully immersive three-dimensional spaces [1][4][5]. At the highest doses, "the room may seem to disappear" as external reality is replaced by the DMT visual landscape [5].

### Entity encounters
A well-documented phenomenon at high doses. Davis et al. (2020) surveyed 2,561 individuals about their "single most memorable entity encounter after taking N,N-dimethyltryptamine" [6]. Key findings:

- Reports of encountering "entities" or "sentient presences" occur in roughly half of high-dose experiences [5]
- "The most common descriptive labels for the entity were being, guide, spirit, alien, and helper" [6]
- 85% reported "extrasensory (e.g. telepathic)" communication [6]
- 75% reported the entity existed "in some real but different dimension or reality" [6]
- 69% reported receiving "a message, task, mission, purpose, or insight" from the encounter [6]

These experiences, while potentially profound or unsettling, are fully transient and resolve completely within minutes.

### Emotional range
High-dose experiences can include "euphoria, calm, fear, and anxiety" as well as "ego dissolution, feeling as if one has died or no longer exists" [4][5]. The experience of fear or anxiety, when it occurs, is also temporary.

### Relationship to acceptance
Community and clinical observations suggest that attempting to resist or "push away" an anxious DMT experience tends to intensify the anxiety, while accepting and "going with" the experience tends to reduce distress [3]. This pattern is well-documented across psychedelic research more broadly.

## Dose Comparison

Aborting doses (5-10 mg) are substantially lower than typical recreational doses. For context:
- A 2024 naturalistic field study examined participants using 40-75 mg inhaled DMT [7]
- Clinical trials typically test 0.2-0.4 mg/kg IV (~14-28 mg for a 70 kg person) [1]
- Entity encounters and breakthrough experiences generally require >25 mg inhaled

The subjective difference between an aborting dose and a breakthrough dose is vast — comparable to the difference between feeling slightly tipsy and being heavily intoxicated.

## DMT Vapor Properties

### Smell
DMT vapor has a distinctive odor. Common descriptions include:
- **Mothball-like** — the most frequently cited comparison [8][9]
- **Burnt plastic or rubber** — especially when overheated [8][9]
- **Floral** — described by some as "a fresh soft floral essence" or "like flowers and pollen," particularly with pure white/translucent crystal [9]

The smell is sometimes described as a combination of these elements. The solid powder itself may have little to no odor; the characteristic smell emerges primarily during vaporization [9].

### Dissipation
Unlike cigarette smoke, DMT vapor dissipates quickly. It does not linger on clothes, furniture, or in rooms for extended periods. The vapor is visible briefly during exhalation but clears within minutes.
