## Oxygen {#oxygen}

Oxygen is the gold standard legal method to abort attacks. If you can access oxygen, strongly consider making it your first-line abortive. 

However, oxygen has drawbacks too:

* Oxygen is expensive. It can be difficult to get an oxygen prescription.  
* Oxygen tanks are bulky. Travelling with them is often impossible.  
* Oxygen often takes more time to abort an attack than DMT, or even triptans.

It can be challenging to make oxygen work for you. Tweaking your protocol is recommended for you to get the best results. While the focus of this guide isn’t oxygen, we recall a few recommendations below.

### Using oxygen as an abortive

*For more information on oxygen as abortive, see: [clusterbusters](https://clusterbusters.org/resource/oxygen-therapy-for-cluster-headaches/), [clusterheadaches.com](http://clusterheadaches.com/O2).*

#### Setup

Your setup should output pure oxygen at rates as high as possible. 10-15 liters per minute is generally the *minimum* recommended flow rate. 

* Oxygen source:  
  * Pure oxygen, stored in a gas cylinder.  
  * Don’t use a concentrator, as it won’t outpure pure oxygen.  
* Oxygen delivery: You can use either  
  * A non-rebreather mask (a mask that covers your nose and mouth, designed to deliver pure oxygen);  
    * The mask or mouthpiece should be tightly sealed. The target is to inhale 100% oxygen. No room air must be inhaled.  
  * A high-flow demand valve ([example](https://www.bprmedical.com/ultraflow-oxygen-demand-valve/)).  
    * Some people find it more effective than a non-rebreather mask.

#### Protocol

As soon as you feel an attack is coming:

* Turn on the pure oxygen flow on the highest setting.  
* Breathe into the mask / the demand valve repeatedly, taking in oxygen as quickly as you can.  
* After a few minutes, you can try turning down the oxygen flow to 6-8 liters per minute.  
* Stay on pure oxygen for at least 5 minutes after the attack is gone.  
* If you feel no improvement after 15-20 minutes, you can try taking a 10 minute break, and applying the protocol again.

**Trial and error is important**. Keep tweaking your protocol, and see what works for you.

## Safety of DMT use {#safety-of-dmt-use}

### Overview

DMT is generally **low-risk** for your physical or mental health:

* DMT isn’t **addictive**  
* **Tolerance and rebounds**: Long-term use of DMT isn’t known to decrease its potential to abort attacks. It’s also not reported to cause rebounds, i.e. to increase the frequency of headache or cluster headache attacks.  
  * More generally, there are no known negative side-effects of long-term DMT use  
* **Overdose and toxicity**: Unlike other substances like alcohol, DMT isn’t toxic: It doesn’t damage the brain or the rest of the body. There are no known deaths clearly attributable to DMT overdosing. When taken recreationally, the dose is substantially lower than any dose that might be risky.[^1] And the dose necessary to abort an attack is even lower. (more)  
  * However, taking DMT increases heart rate and blood pressure. DMT can also be dangerous *in combination* with other drugs or medicines. ([more](#bookmark=kix.if6jk4xlamif))  
* **Psychological effects**: At doses typically necessary to abort attacks, DMT will likely only have a mild effect on your perceptions, your feelings and your emotions. At 2 to 10 times higher doses, DMT may distinctly alter your perception of the world, and may induce pleasant or unpleasant states of mind. At higher doses still, you’ll have a very intense trip (see Table below).  
* **Duration** of the effects: When vaped and [not combined with specific drugs](#interactions-with-other-drugs), DMT is quickly eliminated by the body. DMT starts acting within seconds, and most of the effects typically disappear after 10-20 minutes.

Here are the main risks of taking DMT:

* **Psychological risks**: DMT consumption can induce fear or anxiety. If taken at high doses and ignoring safety protocols, the experience induced by DMT can be traumatizing.  
  * However, severe psychological effects such as psychosis are *extremely unlikely*. That said, avoid taking DMT if you or anyone in your immediate family suffers from a psychotic disorder (e.g., schizophrenia). ([more](#physiological-and-psychological-effects-of-dmt))  
  * Starting with small doses and following safety protocols is crucial to maximize the chance you have a good experience. ([more](#aborting-protocol:-“puff-wait-and-repeat”))  
* **Interactions** with other drugs or medications.  
  * You cannot take DMT if you’re on lithium, as seizure is a real risk. And DMT combined with other drugs or medications might trigger serotonin syndrome. ([more](#interactions-with-other-drugs))  
* **Increased blood pressure**. DMT temporarily increases blood pressure and heart rate. Even at high doses (50 mg, 10 times more than recommended to abort attacks), this temporary increase in blood pressure is unlikely to pose a significant health risk.   
  * You might want to exercise caution if your cardiovascular system is already under stress, e.g. because you take triptans (see [here](#triptans)).

### Interactions with other drugs {#interactions-with-other-drugs}

DMT may be dangerous when combined with other drugs. **If you take lithium (sold under**  
**brand names like Lithobid or Eskalith), do not take DMT — the combination can**  
**cause seizures**.

If you’re taking or have recently taken (within the past few weeks) any supplement or medication, check for dangerous interactions before taking DMT. Do thorough research, and **talk first to your doctor** if possible. Have a [sitter](#sitter), especially when taking DMT for the first few times.

The main interaction risks are:

* **Seizure**, when combined with **lithium**. **Don’t take DMT if you also take lithium.** You might be taking lithium to treat bipolar disorder.   
* **Serotonin syndrome** risk with certain **serotonergic drugs** (drugs that affect the brain's [serotonin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serotonin) levels), such as MAOIs or SSRIs.  
  * Serotonin syndrome is a serious condition caused by too much serotonin, with symptoms like high fever, muscle twitching, abnormal heartbeat, and confusion.   
  * While the risk with DMT is generally low, it critically depends on any other drugs you are taking or have taken in the recent weeks. ## Aborting with DMT {#aborting-with-dmt}

This section provides step-by-step instructions for preparing and using a DMT vape to abort an attack. If you've never used a vape or handled a psychedelic substance before, that’s okay. This guide will walk you through the process clearly and safely.

Before attempting to abort an attack with DMT you’ll need to:

1. Get the necessary equipment and prepare your vape;  
2. Have a first DMT experience **outside of an attack**, in a controlled environment, and **with a sitter.**

We detail those steps below.

### Preparing the vape {#preparing-the-vape}

*(Note: We won’t cover e-mesh delivery here. It can be more fiddly and isn’t recommended to start with.)*

#### Choosing a vape pen

We recommend a pen with the following characteristics:

* **Battery**: Must have variable voltage to allow for temperature control.  
  * *Why:* Too low temperature, and you won’t get enough DMT to abort your attack efficiently. Too high, and the molecule will burn.  
* **Cartridge:** High-quality **ceramic coil** cartridge.  
  * Why: Ceramic heats evenly and reduces burning.

Some clusterheads recommend the following models:

* [CCell Fino](https://www.ccell.com/battery/fino)  
* [Coolfire Z60](https://www.innokin.com/coolfire-z60) with 0.3 Ohm coils

#### Preparing the carrier liquid

**Getting a carrier liquid**. Get a nicotine-free carrier liquid (aka “vape juice”), e.g. [this one](https://www.thevapechase.co.uk/products/vimtoz-by-mad-king-50ml-0mg-50-50). The liquid is made of two ingredients: propylene glycol (PG) and vegetable glycerin (VG). We suggest a 50% PG / 50% VG ratio. 

**Mixing in DMT**. Unless you acquired your DMT mixed with the carrier liquid already, you'll now need to add DMT powder (also called “freebase DMT") to the liquid. We recommend a 1:1 DMT to liquid ratio, i.e., 1 gram of DMT per gram liquid. The mixing procedure is detailed [here]().

**Additional details**:  
 

1. **PG:VG ratio**. While we suggest a 1:1 PG to VG ratio, you can experiment with other ratios too. Higher PG concentration dissolves DMT better (lowering the chance that it turns back into solid crystals inside the device), while higher VG concentration makes the vapor less rough on the throat.  
2. **Unclogging your vape**. Your vape might clog if DMT re-crystallizes inside of it. You can find unclog instructions [here](#bookmark=id.qel9ag1ox2ch).  
3. **DMT:liquid ratio**. You’re encouraged to experiment with other DMT to liquid ratios. Some people prefer a 1:3 ratio.

### Preparing to abort with DMT {#preparing-to-abort-with-dmt}

#### Mindset and environment

**Get a first DMT experience when you’re in a good place**. We strongly recommend you take DMT for the first time:

* Outside of an attack  
  * Follow the same [protocol](#aborting-protocol:-“puff-wait-and-repeat”) as if you were trying to abort. Start with a small dose, and only take additional doses if you feel comfortable.  
* In a relaxed state of mind. For this first practice session, pick a time when you’re feeling calm and in a good headspace.  
  * Doing breathwork or light exercise beforehand can be a good idea, see e.g. [here](https://substack.com/inbox/post/177860105).  
* In an environment that’s familiar and where you feel safe and good.  
* Ideally with a sitter (see below).

#### Sitter {#sitter}

**Take DMT with a sitter**, at least for the first time. A sitter is a sober person who’s aware that you’re taking DMT and is ready to support you if needed. The sitter should:

* Understand how DMT acts and what its bodily and psychological effects can be.  
* Be aware of [safety](#safety-of-dmt-use) precautions. In particular, understand what could cause a seizure or a serotonin syndrome, what the symptoms are, and what to do.  
* If needed, provide safety, comfort, or soothing.   
* (Optionally) Log your protocol and the results.

### Aborting protocol: “puff-wait-and-repeat” {#aborting-protocol:-“puff-wait-and-repeat”}

This protocol takes advantage of the fact that inhaled DMT acts very fast (within seconds). You do not need to take a large dose upfront. You can take small doses one after another ("stacking" them) until the pain breaks. Stacking small doses helps you get used to how DMT feels, and minimizes the risk of having a bad time. 

Before your first time, make sure you've read the [preparation section](#preparing-to-abort-with-dmt) above and have a [sitter](#sitter) present. Also read the section on [what to do if you have a bad experience](#bookmark=kix.69abmf4mtr7h).

We encourage you to experiment with the settings (DMT to carrier liquid ratio, temperature, how deep you inhale, etc) and to log everything precisely. Over time, this will help you adjust the protocol to abort your attacks as efficiently as possible.

1. Check your pen is charged. Pre-heat it to 200–220°C (392–428°F)  
   * You want the pen to be hot enough to vaporize DMT but not burn it.  
2. Stay seated or reclined  
3. Puff (first dose)  
   * Draw vapor from the pen for 2-3 seconds  
   * Remove the pen from your mouth and breathe in fresh air to fill your lungs completely  
   * Hold breath for 5-7 seconds, then exhale  
4. Wait and assess  
   * Wait 30 seconds  
   * Has the pain dropped? Do you feel a "body buzz" (a warm tingling sensation)?  
   * Log: device and settings used, time from inhale to first relief, any side effects  
5. Repeat (if needed)  
   * If pain persists take another puff  
   * To avoid tripping, consider stopping when you feel a “body buzz” 

**In case of bad experience**

It’s possible that you don’t enjoy the ride. If that’s the case, remind yourself: "This is a normal drug effect. It will end in 10-20 minutes." In addition, we recommend you try:

1. Grounding  
* Physical grounding: Feel the contact of your body with the physical surroundings.   
* Breathwork: Breathe slowly. Count your breaths from 1 to 4\.  
2. Leaning into the experience, minimizing resistance  
   * Don’t try to resist; this could create a self-reinforcing loop of panic.  
   * Instead go with the flow. Open up to the experience, whatever it is, and accept it fully.

# Serotonin syndrome {#serotonin-syndrome}

Serotonin syndrome occurs when the brain is flooded with serotonin. Symptoms may include dilated pupils, shivering, sweating, an increased heart rate and abnormal heartbeat, fever, as well as jerking or twitching. (see [below](#symptoms-and-what-to-do))

In the event of severe serotonin syndrome, **it’s important that it is correctly identified as such** by the medical professionals who assist you. So, it’s important to understand when you’re most at risk, and what the symptoms are.

Serotonin syndrome is *dose-dependent*. That means it’s difficult to tell in advance whether a combination of drugs will be risky or not: It depends on the dose taken, and on the timing. That’s why we advise you, before taking DMT, to **talk to your doctor about everything you’re taking (including supplements and herbs), and to be with a sitter at least the first few times you take DMT**.

## Symptoms and what to do {#symptoms-and-what-to-do}

If, after taking DMT while on one or several other serotonergic drugs, you suspect you have serotonin syndrome, **call your healthcare provider**, and don’t take more serotonergic drugs (including DMT). If the symptoms are severe or rapidly worsening, immediately call the emergency services.

**Immediately call emergency services if you have any of the following severe symptoms**:

* Fainting / unconsciousness   
* High fever (greater than 101.3 degrees Fahrenheit, or 38.5 degrees Celsius)  
* Tremor (involuntary movements) or severe muscle rigidity  
* Fast heart rate / irregular heartbeat, plus feeling very unwell  
* Seizure  
* Milder symptoms that are rapidly worsening  ￼

Other serotonin syndrome symptoms include:

* Agitation or restlessness  
* Insomnia  
* Confusion  
* Rapid heart rate  
* Dilated pupils  
* Loss of muscle coordination or twitching muscles  
* High blood pressure  
* Muscle rigidity  
* Heavy sweating  
* Diarrhea  
* Headache  
* Shivering  
* Goose bumps

For more information, you can consult:

## Causes of serotonin syndrome {#causes-of-serotonin-syndrome}

Serotonin syndrome is most likely when two or more serotonergic agents are combined, especially when they act via different mechanisms. You’re most at risk of serotonin syndrome risk when DMT is taken in combination with:

1. MAOIs ([see below](#maois));  
   1. Typically taken for treatment-resistant depression, or as psychedelic plant brews.  
2. Multiple other drugs whose serotonergic effects stack up ([see below](#combinations-with-more-than-two-drugs)).  
   1. For example: DMT \+ an SSRI \+ dextromethorphan.

### MAOIs {#maois}

The highest-risk involves pairing DMT with MAOIs (monoamine oxidase inhibitors). MAOIs slow down the elimination of serotonin by the body. That means serotonin can build up to a high level, triggering the syndrome. 

Because MAOIs prolong the effect of DMT, it’s possible for the serotonin syndrome to develop or worsen several hours after DMT is taken.

MAOIs also turns DMT into a more potent psychedelic: You might have a much more intense and longer trip than on the same dose without MAOIs. This in itself can be psychologically tolling.

Common MAOIs include:

* Certain antidepressants  
  * Notably: phenelzine, tranylcypromine, isocarboxazid and moclobemide  
* Certain antibiotics  
  * Notably isoniazid and linezolid  
* Plant-based MAOIs like ayahuasca brews, Syrian rue, harmine/harmaline blends (“changa” can include these)

### Combinations with more than two drugs {#combinations-with-more-than-two-drugs}

A serotonin syndrome might be triggered by a combination of DMT \+ two or more drugs that elevate serotonin levels. Even if each drug is safe in isolation, their effects can stack up, especially if they elevate serotonin each in a different way. 

There are many serotonergic drugs. Most of them are used as antidepressants or painkillers. The main categories are:

* *Serotonin reuptake inhibitors*. This category includes:  
  * SSRIs  
    * Notably: citalopram, escitalopram, fluoxetine, fluvoxamine, paroxetine, and sertraline  
  * SNRIs  
    * Notably: venlafaxine, tramadol (also an opioid)  
  * Certain TCAs (tricyclic antidepressants):  
    * Notably: clomipramine and imipramine   
  * Certain opioids:  
    * Notably: tramadol, methadone, meperidine, fentanyl  
    * (Morphine is *not* thought to have a dangerous interaction)  
  * Certain antihistamines:   
    * Notably: chlorpheniramine and brompheniramine  
  * Dextromethorphan   
  * Certain plants  
    * Notably: St John’s wort  
* *Serotonin releasers*. This category includes:  
  * Amphetamine  
  * Ecstasy (3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine)

## Triptans {#triptans}

#### Serotonin syndrome

Triptans are thought to be unlikely to contribute to serotonin syndrome (see e.g. [here](https://www.cfp.ca/content/64/10/720), [here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triptan#Interactions)). Caution is advised nonetheless, as they interact with the serotonin system.

#### Cardiovascular risks

Taking DMT shortly after triptans can significantly stress the heart. This is because triptans narrow blood vessels, and DMT can increase heart rate and blood pressure.   
If a triptan was taken, a washout period of at least 24 hours (roughly 5 half-lives for most triptans) is safer before attempting DMT. 

# Physiological and psychological effects of DMT {#physiological-and-psychological-effects-of-dmt}

DMT hasn’t been very much researched yet. So, we lack scientific data about what the psychological effects of DMT at different dosages might be. That said, the evidence we have so far points to DMT being:

* **Extremely unlikely to trigger severe psychological effects, such as psychosis**. This is already extremely unlikely at high doses (50 mg or more), and even less likely at doses typically sufficient to abort attacks (5-10 mg). ([more](#severe-psychological-effects:-more-details))  
  * It’s important to note that people suffering from psychotic disorders (like schizophrenia) are usually excluded from clinical trials. So, we know very little about the risks for them. If you’re in that situation, we recommend you exercise caution. Talk to your doctor, and don’t take DMT if you’re not absolutely sure it’s safe for you.  
* **Likely to cause non-dangerous (but potentially unpleasant) effects** such as feeling cold, feeling your heart racing, being anxious, or seeing fractal patterns. ([more](#non-severe-effects:-more-details))  
  * Whether the effects will be pleasant or not critically depends on your environment and your mindset.

## Severe psychological effects: more details {#severe-psychological-effects:-more-details}

The scientific evidence we have on DMT points to a **safe psychological profile**. Here are for example results pulled out from [two recent](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamapsychiatry/fullarticle/2822968) [meta-analyses](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-024-02800-5):

* **In clinical studies with healthy volunteers:** None of the clinical studies analyzed in the two meta-analyses reports severe adverse events (i.e., symptoms that require hospitalization or are life-threatening \-- this include, but is not limited to, psychosis), either immediately upon taking DMT, or later.   
* **Among ayahuasca users:** In a population study[^2] of users of ayahuasca (a herbal decoction containing DMT), persistent psychotic problems were estimated to be exceptionally rare (1 in 50,000 cases). Similar numbers were reported in a population study of users of peyote (a plant containing another psychedelic, mescaline).  
* **In clinical studies with psychotic patients:** We lack clinical data regarding DMT. That said, historical data (1950s-70s) on administering LSD[^3] to patients with schizophrenia suggests a risk of psychedelic-induced psychosis of about 3.8% in that specific population. In almost all cases no long-lasting psychotic symptoms were observed.

It’s furthermore important to note that, in many studies, the dose is **much higher than what’s sufficient to abort attacks** (typically at least 50 mg, compared to 5-10 mg).

## Non-severe effects: more details {#non-severe-effects:-more-details}

It’s common for DMT users to experience a variety of non-dangerous effects, ranging from pleasant to unpleasant. **All effects are generally reported to start after a few seconds, to peak around 1 minute, and disappear after 10-20 minutes.**

On a 5 to 10 mg dose, which is most likely sufficient to abort an attack, you’re likely to:

* Feel your heart racing, as if you just climbed stairs quickly.  
* Feel cold, and perhaps sweat.

You might also feel dizzy, lightheaded or nauseous, or have difficulty moving your limbs. Those symptoms are part of the normal bodily response to an increase in serotonin activity in the brain. They’ll disappear after 10-20 minutes. If they don’t, be mindful of the [serotonin syndrome](#serotonin-syndrome), and take action if necessary.   
You can consult the two tables [here](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Rbid86bUAh0uZE5azsC4i81UszpmavoUgVLSyqB-nHM/edit?usp=sharing) for a more comprehensive list of bodily symptoms (not limited to small doses).

Your perceptions and feelings will also likely be slightly altered:

* You’ll likely feel a warm or buzzing sensation in the body. You might also feel like gravity suddenly increased.  
* You might see visual patterns (such as fractals or mandala-like patterns) with eyes closed.   
* You might hear a high-pitched ringing sound.

You might also feel nervous or anxious. If it’s the case, remember that the effects will be gone within 10-20 minutes.

Stronger effects will appear at higher doses:

* Visual patterns with eyes open, or even a complete disappearance of the room you’re in  
* Strong feelings, e.g. of meaningfulness, of kindness or of fear.  
* Seeing or feeling the presence of an entity (sometimes referred to as an “encounter”).   
  * The encounter is most often reported to be positive (benevolent or guiding entity) to neutral. In \~10% of the cases, the encounter is perceived as negative.  
* A sense of dissociation from your body, or a sense of a loss of self (ego death)

* **The Ingredients:**  
  * **DMT Crystal:** (Freebase form).  
  * **The Liquid Base:** 100% Propylene Glycol (PG) **OR** a mix of PG and Vegetable Glycerin (VG).  
    * *Recommendation:* A **70% PG / 30% VG** mix or **50/50** is best. Pure PG dissolves crystal best but is harsh; adding a little VG smooths it out.  
* **The Tools:**  
  * Small **Shot Glass** (glass is mandatory; plastic may melt or leach).  
  * **Blunt-Tip Syringe** (1ml or larger).  
  * A bowl of **Hot Water** (from the tap is fine; boiling is risky).  
  * **Stirring Tool** (Stainless steel dab tool or a clean paperclip).

---

#### **2\. The Process (Step-by-Step)**

**Step A: Measure**

1. Weigh your DMT. Let's assume you are using **1 gram**.  
2. Measure your liquid using the syringe.  
   * **For the 1:1 Ratio (Standard):** Measure exactly **1mL** of your PG/VG liquid.  
   * *Note:* If you are worried about harshness, you can start with 0.7mL of PG, dissolve the crystal, and then add 0.3mL of VG.

**Step B: Combine**

1. Place the **1g of crystal** into the bottom of the dry shot glass.  
2. Squirt the **1mL of liquid** directly onto the crystals.  
3. *Observation:* It will look slushy, cloudy, and clumpy. It will not dissolve yet.

**Step C: The Heat Bath**

1. Fill a bowl with **hot water** (approx 60°C–70°C / 140°F–160°F). It should be hot to the touch but not boiling.  
2. Place the **shot glass** into the water. Ensure the water level is high enough to warm the mixture but **low enough that no water splashes inside** the shot glass. Water will ruin the solution.  
3. Let it sit for 2–3 minutes to warm up.

**Step D: Stir and Dissolve**

1. As the mixture warms, the liquid will become thinner (less viscous).  
2. **Stir gently** with your metal tool.  
3. Keep stirring until the liquid is **completely clear** and amber/yellow.  
   * *The "Light Check":* Hold the glass up to a light. If you see *any* floating specks, clouds, or swirls, it is **not done**. Put it back in the heat and stir longer.  
   * *Why this matters:* Even tiny undissolved crystals will get sucked into the cartridge's intake holes and clog the coil, rendering the pen useless during an attack.

**Step E: Transfer to Cartridge**

1. While the liquid is still warm and thin, suck it all up into the **syringe**.  
2. Inject it into the **cartridge** tank.  
   * *Tip:* Slide the needle down the side of the glass to let the oil settle at the bottom. Avoid injecting it into the center chimney (airflow tube).  
3. Screw the mouthpiece on immediately.

**Step F: The Wicking (Crucial)**

1. **Do NOT use the pen yet.**  
2. Let the filled cartridge sit upright for **at least 30 minutes** (ideally 1 hour).  
   * *Reason:* The ceramic coil needs time to absorb the thick liquid. If you fire the button while the ceramic is dry, you will burn the coil instantly, and the cartridge will taste like burnt rubber forever.

---

#### **Troubleshooting: "It Recrystallized\!"**

If you leave the pen in a cold room, you might see the liquid turn solid or "slushy" with white crystals. This is normal for 1:1 ratios.

* **Do not throw it away.**  
* **The Fix:**  
  1. Put the cartridge into a waterproof Ziploc bag.  
  2. Submerge the bag in a cup of hot water for 5 minutes.  
  3. Take it out and shake it. The heat will re-dissolve the crystals, and it will be clear again.  
  4. *Prevention:* Store your pen at room temperature, not in a cold car or basement.
